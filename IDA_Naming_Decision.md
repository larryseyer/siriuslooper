Both products live under **AutomagicArt**. Both are personified character names following the AUTO → OTTO palindromic transformation principle that established the brand naming convention. Drummer and bandleader — two musicians, two roles, two products in a coherent brand world.

## Why Each Word Earns Its Place

The backronym maps to load-bearing chapters in the Sirius Looper whitepaper (V7):

**Idea** — §1.1: *"A loop is an idea... a musical idea, captured in a form that allows it to be heard again."* The whitepaper's deepest noun. The thesis the entire architecture exists to serve.

**Development** — §10.5 names *theme and development* as one of the five first-class grammatical phrase relationships the architecture treats as data, not interpretation. Phrases *develop* other phrases as a structural property of the system. Beyond that single section, the entire architecture supports musical development as its central operation: the always-running tape (§8.1) captures ideas; retroactive capture (§C.2) extracts and refines them; the Constituent hierarchy (Part IX) holds them across edits; repetition rules (Part XII) let them vary; arrangement (Part XIII) develops them into songs. "Development" is also a musician's word — *"I'm developing this idea into a song"* is how the work actually gets described. It names what IDA *does* to an idea, in the vocabulary a musician would use.

**Arranger** — Part XIII (Arrangement and Narrative). §1.6: *"arrangement is creation, not processing... the looper is responsible for arrangement at every level of the Constituent hierarchy."* The looper's central act named explicitly. An *arranger* is a real musical role — the person who decides how the song unfolds, who plays when, how phrases sequence into sections.

## Personal Meaning

IDA carries the name of Larry Seyer's mother, **Ida Mae**. The product is named IDA (not Ida Mae) because the three-letter form reads as a product where the longer form reads as a person. The story lives in the about page, the launch communications, and the documentation — not in the name itself.

This is the kind of meaning that outlasts a founder. A brand that honors family without explaining itself.

## Trademark Status

**No audio software product called IDA was found** in available searches. The audio software space appears genuinely clear — the cleanest of any of the eight candidates evaluated (Reel, Spool, Phrase, Rita, Lira, KOTO, Ida, others).

**Existing IDA marks, all in unrelated software categories:**

- **IDA Pro by Hex-Rays** — reverse engineering / binary disassembly software. Class 9 but completely different vertical (security/malware research). Different customers, different distribution, different price point. USPTO "relatedness of goods" test favors coexistence between audio software and reverse-engineering software.
- **IDA by 9362-5739 Québec Inc.** (Serial 97716451, filed Dec 2022) — dating/matchmaking mobile app. Class 9 but completely unrelated category.

**Risk profile:** Low, not zero. Hex-Rays is a sophisticated brand owner that may monitor IDA filings even in unrelated categories. They could file an opposition to protect brand breadth, even if unlikely to ultimately succeed.

**Required next step before filing:** Engage a trademark attorney for a proper clearance search. Budget $500-$2,000 for TESS + common-law + state + Madrid Protocol search plus an opinion letter. This is genuinely worth it given the personal weight of the name.

## Domain Strategy

**Canonical home:** `automagicart.com/ida`

No vanity domain needed. AutomagicArt is the established brand umbrella; products live as paths under it. This is the Ableton model — ableton.com hosts Live, Push, Note as paths, not as separate vanity domains.

**Optional later:** If a vanity URL becomes desirable, likely options at standard registrar prices:
- `ida.fm` — .fm carries strong audio/music association (Last.fm)
- `ida.audio` — explicit category signal
- `ida.app` — generic but recognizable

Skip `ida.com` (almost certainly held by an investor at five-figure pricing; unnecessary).

OTTOdrums.com (already purchased) functions similarly — as a marketing landing page and discovery aid, redirecting to `automagicart.com/otto` as the canonical home.

## Decision Trail (Brief)

The naming exercise tested eight serious candidates against three criteria: (a) fidelity to the looper whitepaper's architectural thesis, (b) trademark/commercial availability in audio software, (c) brand coherence with OTTO and AutomagicArt.

- **Sirius Looper / Serious Looper** — dropped. "Sirius" conflicts with Sirius XM trademarks; "Looper" is widely registered in audio software; the looper-as-category framing contradicts the whitepaper's central thesis.
- **Phrase** — strong candidate, killed by collision with OTTO's existing "Phrase Tab" UI terminology.
- **Reel** — strong concept, killed by Reel Audio Instruments (active commercial product in audio plugin space) and REELCRAFTER registration.
- **Spool** — killed by SPOOLER registration for audio software (Serial 97041742).
- **Lira / KOTO** — strong personified backronyms, not fully checked but acceptable runners-up.
- **Rita** — killed by RiTA from Global Audio Solutions (existing pro-audio product) and RITA trademark registration for music recordings.
- **IDA** — selected. Audio software space clear; personal meaning; backronym maps cleanly to whitepaper chapters; pairs with OTTO as drummer/bandleader.

The construction principle that finally produced IDA: **a four- or three-letter backronym whose words each map to load-bearing whitepaper chapters, that reads as a human name, and that pairs with OTTO as a character in a band.** This principle is now AutomagicArt's product-naming convention going forward.
